Dennis Ritchie

Timeline

1968: Dennis Ritche graduated from Harvard University in physics and mathematics

1970: Created the C programming language and codeveloped the UNIX operating system

1983: Awarded the ACM turing reward

1988: Elected to the US National Academy of Engineering

1990: Appointed head of system software development and research at Bell Labs

1999: Awarded the US National Medal of Technology

2007: Retired from Bell Labs

2011: Passed away in New Jersey, United States

Contribution

Dennis Ritchie's work is the foundation of the modern day digital world. His development of the C programming language helps programming and technology work better today. Ritchie proves his skills by leading development on the Inferno and Plan 9 operating systems. He was also key to development of the UNIX operating system, which is influential to today's computing. To conclude, Dennis Ritchie's efforts have been a great influence and contribution to today's programming.
